ABOUT US

The UC Berkeley chapter of Phi Beta Lambda is one of the largest business organizations on campus. Our organization revolves around two central tenets: the promotion of the skills necessary for success in business leadership, and the opportunity to experience the benefits of a small club that is often lacking at a large university such as UC Berkeley. All members of Berkeley PBL are exposed to and instilled with critical skills and knowledge that possess value during and after college life.

Hands-on Approach

We believe in learning through practice. Each of our 10 committees take on unique projects each semester and will arm you with not only the tools needed to succeed in the field of your choice, but also building blocks to create your professional portfolio.

Small Club Feel

Despite having over 90 active members each semester, we ensure that we retain the same sort of culture that a small club would have. Joining PBL isn’t just being a member of another organization; it’s being part of a team. We study, eat, compete, travel, work, and play together and truly build a “home away from home.”

Work Hard, Play Smart

We believe that while preparing for life after college is important, our short 4 years here are just as significant. When we’re not organizing campus-wide events or providing strategic advising for Bay Area companies, our priorities including having fun, meeting new people, and making the most of that college experience.

Phi Beta Lambda is an organization that is unique because of the combination of opportunities we offer and the dedication we have towards your personal and professional growth to be the next business leaders.

- Raymond Tong, Fall 2014 President

Nationally Recognized

Being a nationally recognized business organization, members have the opportunities to compete at state and national levels, attend various leadership workshops, and network with members from other universities.

ASUC and Haas Sponsored

As an ASUC and HAAS sponsored organization, PBL Berkeley has access to exclusive campus resources.

10 Specialized Committees

We provide members the opportunity to learn from different aspects of business such as Consulting, Finance, and Marketing. Scroll down to learn more about the committees.

Diverse

While we are a business organization, we have members from all majors, disciplines, interests, and ages.

Committees

Community Service

The Community Service Committee aims to show members that community service plays a role in businesses, rather than businesses playing a role in community service. Through involvement in energy ambassador programs to participating in the Berkeley Project, this committee strives to build relationships not only within our club but also within our community.

Consulting

The Consulting Committee was established to integrate leadership development with practical experience in the industry. With knowledge gained from consulting frameworks and case studies, members will work directly with an external client to craft strong consulting recommendations. Through consulting projects and workshops, the committee members will strive to become polished public speakers, analytical researchers, and versatile problem-solvers.

Finance

Our goal is to provide the theoretical, technical, and professional resources necessary to give members the option to pursue a career in finance. By the end of the semester, members will not only have obtained the knowledge they need to succeed, but also have created a project portfolio that can be utilized for recruitment purposes.

Historian

The Historian Committee documents the events of PBL. It helps create, grow, and preserve memories through creative photography and videography projects.

Marketing

The Marketing Committee introduces members to advertising and strategic marketing as well as gives them the opportunity to practice their case analysis skills. This committee also creates marketing campaigns for large-scale events hosted by Phi Beta Lambda to Berkeley students through the use of strategic analysis and proven advertising techniques.

Professional Development

The Professional Development Committee provides a valuable, enriching, and recruitment centered experience for members to apply themselves in PBL and beyond to the professional world.

Publications

The Publications Committee equips members with a basic understanding of the Adobe Creative Suite programs. Through a variety of workshops, both technical and theory-based, members will be introduced to design in a business setting. Members will also have the chance to create the yearbook, which showcases members and events every semester.

Social

The Social Committee is responsible for bonding members through professionally planned and executed events. On top of intra-committee bonding, the committee aims to preserve and promote the tight-knit relations that PBL is known for. The curriculum is catered to what members want most, such as event planning experience, public speaking and organizational skills.

Internal Networking

The Internal Networking chairs are dedicated to creating connections and bonds between all members of PBL, no matter what committee they come from or whether they are officers, CMs, or executives. The Internal Networking committee specializes in creating these bonds and lasting memories and friendships which will help shape your college experience for this semester and beyond. From organizing casual hangouts to structured group activities to grabbing a quick meal, we’ll do our best to make this a fun semester for everyone, and to encourage all to make meaningful and long-lasting connections as a part of Phi Beta Lambda.

Web Development

The Web Development committee is a new initiative in Berkeley PBL. For the upcoming semester, we plan on rebuilding and restructuring both our internal and external websites. Additionally we hope to implement new features that will make administrative tasks run much smoother and enrich your PBL experience. We also plan to construct a foundation and system in which a larger team of individuals can work on the website collaboratively and efficiently. Our vision for the future is to eventually have multiple people be able to work together on maintaining and building PBL’s tech tools. However, for Spring 2015 we will not be taking committee members as we are spending the semester making preparations to allow for committee members in the following semesters.

Berkeley Case Competition Program

PBL is pleased to announce that it will be a technical sponsor for a Case Competition Program from February 25 to March 8, 2015. This program offers undergraduate students an opportunity to study real-life cases and develop professional skills by analyzing business challenges. Anyone who is currently studying at an accredited four-year university or college, regardless of previous business or case competition experience, is welcome to participate. For more information about the Case Competition Program, please visit www.berkeleycasecomp.org
